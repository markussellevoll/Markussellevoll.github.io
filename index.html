What is Cognitive Science?

Cognitive science is the interdisciplinary study of the mind and its processes, exploring how humans think, learn, and solve problems. At its core, it combines insights from fields such as psychology, neuroscience, philosophy, linguistics, anthropology, and computer science to understand cognition. A key focus in cognitive science is the use of programming and logic to model mental processes.

For instance, artificial intelligence (AI) and machine learning are tools often used to replicate or simulate human reasoning and learning in computational systems. Logical frameworks, such as propositional and predicate logic, are essential for analyzing thought processes and building algorithms that mimic human decision-making.

Beyond programming and logic, cognitive science also delves into language acquisition, perception, memory, and problem-solving. It examines how the brain processes information, how we learn languages, and how emotions influence decision-making. Cognitive science also investigates the relationship between the physical brain and abstract thought, integrating neuroscience to study neural mechanisms. By bridging these domains, cognitive science not only helps us understand the nature of human cognition but also drives advancements in technology, such as the development of smarter AI, user-friendly interfaces, and applications in education, healthcare, and beyond.

More About My Studies

The Bachelor's program in Cognitive Science is a three-year full-time study starting in August, totaling 180 ECTS credits.

Structure of the Program:
- First and Second Year: Students complete mandatory courses covering foundational topics in cognitive science, programming, formal logic, statistics, linguistics, philosophy of mind, and biological and cognitive psychology.
- Third Year: Students specialize in one of three fields: Informatics, Information Science, or Philosophy.

Semester Overview:
1st Semester: Introduction to cognitive science, Examen facultatum (language and communication), and an introduction to programming.
2nd Semester: Object-oriented programming, formal logic, deduction and metalogic, and statistics for cognitive research.
3rd Semester: Algorithms and data structures, language and cognition, and knowledge representation and reasoning.
4th Semester: Examen philosophicum, philosophy of mind, and biological and cognitive psychology.
5th and 6th Semesters: Specialization in the chosen field (informatics, information science, or philosophy).

This multidisciplinary program combines theoretical and practical knowledge, providing a comprehensive understanding of cognition and its applications in technology and philosophy.
